The Problem

Blind students want to read.
They just don't get the chance.

285M

people globally live with visual impairment, including 19 million children under 14 (NIH).

<10%

of published books are ever converted into accessible formats like Braille.

1 in 5

preschool-aged children has a vision problem — yet accessible fiction remains almost non-existent.

What's Missing
  • Most Braille books available are textbooks, religious texts, or old translations that feel disconnected from young lives.
  • Reading is treated as education — never as joy — for blind students.
  • Fiction and short stories in Braille are especially rare because they are seen as "non-essential."
  • In many blind schools, students share one Braille book among multiple students, or rely solely on teachers reading aloud.
  • This creates a reading gap — not because blind students don't want to read, but because they don't have access.
Our Solution

"Original stories. Professional Braille. Zero cost."

Create original, engaging short stories written specifically for enjoyment — not curriculum, not achievement.
Convert each story into Braille via certified external transcription services, ensuring professional Grade 1 or Grade 2 quality.
Distribute free of charge to blind schools, libraries, and NGOs — removing every financial barrier.
Stories are not about blindness — they are normal, universal tales every child would love. Inclusion through story.

How We Do It

A simple, repeatable model
built to grow.

01
Content Creation

Writers craft original short stories — tales of adventure, friendship, curiosity — that any child would love, regardless of sight.

02
Braille Production

Certified Braille transcription partners convert each story into Grade 1 or Grade 2 Braille, tailored to each school's level.

03
Distribution

We collaborate with blind schools and NGOs to donate books directly to school libraries and individual students.

04
Feedback Loop

Teachers and students share what resonated. Every response shapes our next story — language, length, themes, tone.
